## Career Arc

### 🎓 Foundation — Clinical Data Management + Early SQL & Analytics *(New Zealand · India)*

Formal training in **clinical data management** — covering data extraction, data storage, data architecture, SAS Base programming, and SDTM standards (the structured data model used in clinical trials and healthcare compliance). This grounded everything that followed in data governance, structured thinking, and working with data that has real-world consequence.

Early roles in New Zealand and India converted that training into hands-on practice — writing SQL to analyze operational data, building reports for business teams, and developing the instinct for what makes data trustworthy and useful.

One of those roles was at **Griffins Foods Limited** — one of New Zealand's largest FMCG and snack manufacturing companies — where data work spanned retail and manufacturing operations. Working in that environment, where supply chain, production, and commercial data intersect, added an operational discipline to the analytical foundation.

During this period, two certifications shaped the delivery mindset that runs through everything since:

- **Six Sigma Green Belt** — formal training in process improvement, defect reduction, and quality measurement. Directly informs how I think about data quality, pipeline reliability, and validating that outputs are actually correct before they reach business consumers.
- **Project Management certification** — structured framework for scoping, planning, and delivering work with defined outcomes. Combined with Six Sigma, this is where delivery discipline came from — not just picked up on the job.

The combination of clinical data governance training, hands-on SQL practice, manufacturing domain exposure, and formal quality and delivery methodology is what made the transition into data engineering and eventually delivery leadership a natural progression.
